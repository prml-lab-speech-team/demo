Fre-GAN: Adversarial Frequency-consistent Audio Synthesis

Ji-Hoon Kim, Sang-Hoon Lee, Ji-Hyun Lee, Seong-Whan Lee

ABSTRACT

Although recent works on neural vocoder have improved the quality of synthesized audio, there still exists a gap in frequency space between generated and ground-truth audio. This results in spectral artifacts such as hissing noise and reverberation and degrades the sample quality. In this paper, we propose Fre-GAN which achieves frequency-consistent audio synthesis with highly improved generation quality. Specifically, we first present resolution-connected generator and resolution-wise discriminators, which help learn various levels of spectral distributions over multiple frequency bands. Additionally, to learn high-frequency components for clear audio, we introduce discrete wavelet transform in the discriminators. In our experiments, Fre-GAN generates high-fidelity waveforms with a gap of only 0.03 MOS compared to the ground-truth audio while outperforming standard models in quality.
