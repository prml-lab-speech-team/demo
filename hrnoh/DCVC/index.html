Non-parallel Duration Controllable Voice Conversion via Frame-level Phoneme Prediction based Information Bottleneck

BLIND

ABSTRACT

Recently, several Voice Conversion (VC) methods using a simple autoencoder with a carefully designed information bottleneck have been studied. However, their performance is highly dependent on an information bottleneck with a suitable downsampling factor. Additionally, such frame-byframe conversion methods cannot convert speaking styles associated with the length of utterance, such as duration. On the other hand, Sequence-to-sequence (Seq2seq) based VC can convert such styles, but it has a slower inference speed due to generating samples in an autoregressive manner. In this paper, we propose a novel Duration Controllable Voice Conversion model, called DCVC, which can transfer both the speaking style and control the duration of the speech through a frame-level phoneme prediction based information bottleneck. In our experiments, DCVC outperforms baseline models with a 3.78 MOS and a 3.83 similarity score. It can also smoothly control the speech duration while achieving an 11x speedup compared with Seq2seq-based VC in terms of the inference speed.
