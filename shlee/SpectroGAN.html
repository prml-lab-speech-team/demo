SpectroGAN: Spectrogram Generation with Adversarial Networks for Speech Synthesis

Abstract

While current Text-To-Speech (TTS) models have shown significant improvement, it is challenging to generate spectrogram from a text with Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) in speech synthesis. In this paper, we present SpectroGAN, the GANs based neural TTS system. We show that it can be possible to train GANs without using reconstruction loss between target and generated mel-spectrogram by introducing 3 kinds of methods: (1) Fixed-length decoder by using character duration extracted from an autoregressive teacher model; (2) Frame-level linguistic condition in discriminator; (3) Frame segmentation in the discriminator training phase. Compared with the previous model, the SpectroGAN achieves comparable results in a Mean Opinion Score (MOS). The SpectroGAN also shows better results than the base model trained only with reconstruction loss. To validate our model, we also perform ablation studies of key components.
