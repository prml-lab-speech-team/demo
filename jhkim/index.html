Few-shot Speaker Adaptation With Geometric Constraints

Abstract

While speaker adaptation is highly desired for end-to-end Text-To-Speech (TTS) system, it requires a large amount of adaptation data for adapting novel speakers to pre-trained multi-speaker TTS system. We present few-shot speaker adaptive TTS system with two geometric constraints, which can generate speech in the voice of novel speakers from only a few samples. We introduce a two-stage learning procedure, where the network is trained for base speakers with a large number of data and then fine-tuned for novel speakers with only a few data. By introducing two geometric constraints, we can extract discriminative speaker embedding for the novel speakers while preserving the embedding space well learned for the base speakers. The experiments show that the proposed method achieves high performance in terms of speech naturalness and speaker similarity to the real speech from the target speaker. Particularly, we demonstrate that the speaker similarity is improved over standard techniques by utilizing subjective and objective metric.
