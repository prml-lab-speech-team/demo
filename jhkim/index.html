Abstract

We present two geometric constraints for few-shot speaker adaptive Text-To-Speech (TTS) system. Here, a TTS model is trained for base speakers with sufficient amount of training data, and then fine-tuned for novel speakers with a few adaptation data using two geometric constraints. By applying two geometric constraints, we can extract discriminative speaker embedding of the novel speakers even from a small amount of adaptation data without contaminating the embedding space well learned for the base speakers. The experiments show that the proposed method achieves high performance in terms of speech naturalness and speaker similarity of synthesized and real speech. Particularly, we demonstrate that the speaker similarity is improved over standard techniques by utilizing subjective and objective evaluation methods.

Model architecture


In this paper, we introduce two geometric constraints for few-shot speaker adaptive TTS system.
  1. Weight-Centric Embedding Clustering, when used with cross-entropy loss, enables speaker embedding of novel speaker to cluster near the speaker weights.
  2. Angular Weights Separation maintains the weights of a novel speaker far from the other speakers.
