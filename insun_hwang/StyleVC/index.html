Additional Figure



     The figure above shows the training strategy learning to perform voice conversion in a training scenario.
Our model, StyleVC, proposes a novelty training strategy that can effectively learn the source's content and target's style information while using target audio.

    We used two experimental models to prove the performance of our proposed method.
The consistency loss and perceptual loss were applied to AutoVC, respectively, and used as experimental models. These two methods were used by referring to previous studies.
    The experimental model applying the consistency loss to AutoVC generated speech naturally, but the conversion performance was poor.
Since only the finally produced spectral features were used, it would have been challenging to learn low and high-level features.
    On the other hand, the experimental model applying Loss Network to AutoVC converted style while generating voice unnaturally.
This is because it is possible to learn high-level features for the source's content and target's style through the loss network.
However, the performance was low because it was not possible to learn features from low to high-level.
    Using our novel training strategy, the decoder learns features for low and high-level.
Therefore, the decoder can reflect the source's content and target's style information while producing speech.
Through this, the conversion performance in the inference scenario was improved. Also, the performance of inter-gender conversion was improved.
