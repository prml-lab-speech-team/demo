DiffProsody: Latent diffusion-based Prosody Generation for Expressive Speech Synthesis with Prosody Conditional Adversarial Training

Hyung-Seok Oh, Sang-Hoon Lee, Seong-Whan Lee

ABSTRACT

Expressive text-to-speech systems have undergone significant improvement with prosody modeling. However, there is still room for improvement in the conventional prosody modeling methods. Most prosody modeling methods utilize vector quantization as an information bottleneck. However, it may induce the cognitive degradation of synthesized speech with smoothed expressiveness. For prosody prediction, previous work used the auto-regressive method to predict the prosody vector, which has a long-term dependency and slow inference problems. To solve these problems, we present DiffProsody, which can synthesize high-quality expressive speech with a latent diffusion-based prosody generator and prosody conditional adversarial training. Diffusion-based prosody generator produces high-quality prosody vectors. Through prosody conditional discriminator, it is possible to generate high-quality speech with more expressive prosody. Furthermore, we adopt the denoising diffusion generative adversarial network to improve the speed of prosody generation. Hence, DiffProsody can generate prosody 16x faster than the conventional diffusion model. Experimental results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method.
