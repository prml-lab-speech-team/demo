StyleVC: Non-parallel Voice Conversion with Adversarial Style Generalization


In-Sun Hwang, Sang-Hoon Lee, Seong-Whan Lee

Abstract



Voice conversion uses two samples to synthesize speech: the source sample is used for content, the target sample is used to style representation. Therefore, it has been progressed to design information flow to disentangle content and style in a speech. However, separated representation is damaged due to passing through sparse subspace. Besides, VC models suffer from the training-inference mismatch problem that only uses one sample in training; in inference, accordingly, the model extracts inappropriate content and style representation and generates low-quality speech. To address this problem, we introduce a StyleVC, which proposes adversarial style generalization. We propose style generalization, which captures global style representation and restricts the model from copying style information. Besides, we further utilize adversarial training to make the model generate more realistic speech. Moreover, we use a pitch predictor to estimate pitch information according to content and style representation. Finally, we demonstrate our proposed model to generate natural speech. The experimental results also show the superiority of our proposed method.

The code will be released.
