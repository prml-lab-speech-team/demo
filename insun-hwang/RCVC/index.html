RCVC: RHYTHM CONTROLLABLE VOICE CONVERSION WITH DYNAMIC INFORMATION BOTTLENECK


In-Sun Hwang, Sang-Hoon Lee, Seong-Whan Lee

Abstract



There has been significant progress in non-parallel many-to-many voice conversion with a simple autoencoder, which can disentangle content and speaker information from the speech. Although rhythm has recently received attention and plays a vital role in determining the characteristics of a speaker, it is not yet utilized well. Moreover, the information bottleneck could degrade separation performance because it does not consider each speaker's different phoneme duration. In this paper, we introduce RCVC, which is a rhythm controllable voice conversion model. First, we propose a duration predictor to predict phoneme sequence length using speaking speed in order to transfer rhythm. Second, we propose a dynamic information bottleneck to generate content embedding where rhythm is removed. The experimental results also show the superiority of our proposed method.
