FRE-PAINTER: AUDIO FREQUENCY INPAINTING WITH ROBUST SPEECH REPRESENTATION LEARNING OF MASKED AUTOENCODER

Seung-Bin Kim, Sang-Hoon Lee, Ha-Yeong Choi, and Seong-Whan Lee

ABSTRACT

This paper presents Fre-Painter, a high-fidelity audio frequency inpainting system which is neural audio super-resolution by utilizing robust speech representation learning with various masking strategies. Recently, masked autoencoder has been found beneficial in learning robust representations of audio for speech classification tasks. Following these studies, we leverage these representations and investigate several masking strategies for neural audio superresolution. For the audio frequency inpainting, we propose upperband masking strategy with initialization of masking token which is simple but efficient for upsampling task. Furthermore, we also present mixed-ratio masking strategy which makes model robust for input speech with various sampling rates. Specifically, the experimental results demonstrate that Fre-Painter outperforms other neural audio super-resolution models.
